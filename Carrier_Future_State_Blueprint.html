Section01

The summary — what the future state is, in one page

Carrier's future state is one Lead Lifecycle Loop — a single, agentic, measurable operating model spanning every lead from intake through registration and into lifetime service. Two intake patterns feed it. Four signal paths keep Carrier in control of outcome data regardless of dealer cooperation.

Journey 1

Online Inquiry

Two flavors. Web Form / Chat: homeowner interacts directly with a Carrier AI agent on carrier.com — conversational chat interface in-browser that captures identity, intent, and consent. MarchEx: homeowner fills a Guided Intake Form on the dealer-locator page (no bot — structured form that captures intent before the phone number is dialed). Both create a structured Lead in Salesforce before the dealer handoff.

Journey 2

Customer Care Call

Homeowner dials 1-800-CARRIER. Voice AI answers on Carrier-controlled Five9 telephony, conducts natural qualification, applies intelligent routing (performance + proximity + availability + emergency signal) to select the best-fit dealer, warm-transfers to the dealer, and creates the structured lead in Salesforce during the call.

Carrier captures the lead. Carrier routes with context.
The dealer delivers. Carrier captures the outcome.

How Carrier captures outcomes — four independent signal paths

Flow-back from dealer to Carrier layers four independent signal paths, ranked by Carrier control. At every lifecycle stage, at least two fire. Carrier is never dependent on any single one. Section 2 traces each path across the full lifecycle.

Signal Path 1 · Strongest Technical

FSM Integration Telemetry

FSM webhooks, MarchEx metadata, Five9 transcripts, Dispatch events. No dealer effort required — signal is the digital exhaust of normal work.

Signal Path 2 · Most Reliable

Homeowner-side signal

Multi-channel homeowner engagement: SMS + email check-ins, homeowner-facing status page, post-contact satisfaction surveys, and Voice AI warm-transfer recovery on dealer miss. Cadence and channel adapt to lead score and homeowner behavior. Dealer cannot gate it. Opt-in driven; degrades gracefully without consent.

Signal Path 3 · Underused

Distributor Channel Integration

Distributor ERP shows equipment shipped to dealer per installation. Triangulates FSM and homeowner signals. Phase 2+ leverage.

Signal Path 4 · Passive (as-is today)

Dealer-controlled disposition

Voluntary dealer reporting in Dispatch or FSM. Kept in the mix as supplementary color. Never the source of truth.

Phase 1 scope boundary This Blueprint covers Phase 1 only: Online Inquiry + Customer Care Call, with the Lead Lifecycle Loop running end-to-end. Connected Portal telemetry (Phase 2), Retail Partner leads (Phase 2), and Dealer Proposal Tool integration (Phase 3) extend this pattern but are not outlined in this report.

Convergence point From Stage 04 (Engagement) onward, both patterns execute identically. The intake surface differs; the Lifecycle Loop does not. This is the consolidation argument — one system, two doors in.
After-hours behavior Intake runs 24/7 — Voice AI answers, Guided Intake Form captures, identity and scoring operate normally. Routing branches: when no dealer is routable (after-hours, weekends, peak queue), the homeowner gets an immediate acknowledgment with a next-business-day SLA and tracked reference. When dealers come online the next morning, queued leads trigger active re-engagement — not passive wait. Priority is time-of-capture + urgency score; no-heat in January clears the queue.
Section03

The signal path matrix — how Carrier never loses visibility

Six lifecycle stages × four signal paths. At every stage from first contact onward, at least two paths fire independently. The only exception is dealer acknowledgment, which is structurally a single-system event. The dealer's reporting discipline determines how rich Signal Path 4 is. It does not determine whether Carrier sees the outcome.

The principle Green cells fire without dealer cooperation. Amber cells depend on voluntary dealer action. From dealer-contact onward, every row has at least two green cells — if Signal Path 4 is silent, the outcome is still captured. Acknowledgment is the one row that relies on a single strong signal (Dispatch / FSM receipt), which is itself automated and does not require dealer effort. If Signal Path 2 is silent (homeowner didn't opt in or didn't respond), Signal Paths 1 and 3 carry the load.
Section04

Data model with traceable attributes end-to-end enable Revenue Attribution objectives

Attribution works because six backbone attributes persist end-to-end. Every entity inherits from the one before it and adds new attributes. For every lead generated under this program, a service event several years in the future traces cleanly back to the originating Lead — by Serial Number → Lead ID → Homeowner. Historical traceability on Carrier's existing customer base depends on a data modeling and cleansing workstream scoped in the shaping sprint.

Salesforce System of Record Carrier's Salesforce instance is the Day 1 system of record for lead data, lifecycle states, and reporting. This Blueprint is designed to build on top of Salesforce — not replace it, not duplicate its data, not substitute a vendor CRM. Every Lead, Engagement, Sale Record, and reporting surface resolves to Salesforce.
On Dispatch Carrier has confirmed Dispatch as the Day 1 lead routing platform and opened the door (Q&A Q16) to eventually replacing it if the vendor's solution provides equivalent capability. Our Phase 1 position: integrate with Dispatch as-is, do not expand its role, keep the architecture open. Routing logic, SLA management, and disposition tracking live in the Carrier-owned decisioning layer (Salesforce-native) — not in Dispatch. This preserves Carrier's option to retire Dispatch in Phase 2+ without re-architecting the solution.

The six backbone attributes — what must persist end-to-end

  1. Homeowner ID Carrier-generated, persistent identity stitched across all channels.
  2. Lead ID The attribution key. Carried on every routing payload, FSM record, PHDB registration.
  3. Serial Number PHDB record. Ties every Lifetime Event back to its originating Lead.
  4. Dealer ID Canonical across Dispatch, MarchEx, Salesforce, FSM, ServiceBench, Distributor ERP.
  5. Source / Channel Set at intake. Never modified. The marketing attribution dimension.
  6. Timestamp fidelity Every event precise and ordered. Attribution logic depends on sequencing.

On SLAs — illustrative, Carrier-configurable Dealer-contact SLAs are not defined in Carrier's current operating model. This Blueprint uses a 2-hour default dealer-response SLA for illustrative purposes — the number is Carrier-configurable, stage-specific (acknowledgment vs. first contact vs. appointment), score-adjusted (high-value leads may get tighter SLAs), and can vary by dealer tier (FAD Presidents Award dealers may opt into tighter SLAs as a differentiator). Establishing baseline SLAs against Carrier's actual response-time data is a shaping-sprint workstream.
